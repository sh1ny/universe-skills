# The Drowned Witness

## Setup

Mara has lived three years under the official story that Theo caused the Blackout Night. A storm exposes a sealed archive capsule at Bellwether Reef, and the first lantern inside carries Theo's voice. His recording proves he was investigating a hidden memory system before he died.

## Rising Action

Mara tracks the archive's branches through old storm-grid routes and therapy records. Each recovered memory complicates the simple version of justice she wants. Some residents were victims. Some chose to forget. Some helped Venn because they believed public panic would sink the town faster than any storm.

Venn tries to contain Mara with procedure, then pressure, then erasure. The closer Mara gets to the original Blackout Night fragment, the more she sees that Theo hid parts of his investigation from her because he feared what truth would do to Dock Six.

## Climax

Mara retrieves the central lantern: a memory from the night Theo died. It shows that Venn diverted power from the storm grid to preserve the archive during the surge, forcing Theo to choose between exposing the archive and keeping the harbor lit. Theo saved the grid but died sealing the evidence away.

## Resolution

Mara refuses to dump every stolen memory onto the public band. Instead, she releases Theo's proof, the Council orders, and a map of the archive while giving victims control over their own fragments. Venn loses authority. Theo's name is cleared, but the town has to decide what repair means when forgetting was part of its survival.
