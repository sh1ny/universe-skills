# Afterimage Archive

## Overview

The Afterimage Archive is an illegal memory-storage system built from banned therapeutic offload hardware. The original devices were designed to help disaster survivors externalize traumatic sensory loops: sound, smell, body memory, and short visual fragments. In Port Kestrel, Councillor Venn's faction repurposed the hardware into a hidden archive beneath Bellwether Reef.

The archive does not store clean videos. It stores partial human recall. A retrieved memory arrives with breath, panic, missing edges, and the emotional bias of the person who lived it.

## Rules & Limitations

**Source:** Memories must be extracted from a living person through a neural offload rig. Consent is legally required, but the archive contains records taken under coercion, sedation, and falsified therapy agreements.

**Storage:** Memory fragments are held in pressure-sealed capsules called lanterns. Each lantern contains one indexed event cluster and enough sensory metadata to reconstruct it for playback.

**Playback:** A viewer can experience a fragment through a visor and haptic band. Playback is immersive but unstable. Strong memories bleed into the viewer's body: cold, breathlessness, vertigo, pain, and emotional aftershock.

**Hard limits:**
- Cannot recover what the subject never perceived
- Cannot guarantee objective truth; memory can misread cause and motive
- Cannot be copied indefinitely without degradation
- Cannot be safely viewed for more than twelve minutes at a time
- Cannot remove a memory from a person without leaving symptoms

**Tell:** Stolen memories leave "second light" in the eyes during playback: a faint duplicate reflection visible in low light. Harbor clinicians once used this as a diagnostic warning.

## History

Memory offload therapy was legal for a brief period after the flood years, when survivors suffered recurring sensory trauma from drownings and fires. It was banned after patients reported memory thinning, misplaced grief, and personality drift. Venn revived the hardware under emergency authority, first as private treatment for storm crews, then as leverage.

Theo Quill found unauthorized archive traffic while auditing the storm grid. His hidden recording suggests the archive was active during the Blackout Night.

## Practitioners

- **Councillor Ilya Venn** - political custodian of the archive
- **Theo Quill** - discovered the hidden system and created counter-evidence
- **Harbor clinicians** - some participated unknowingly through falsified therapy orders
- **Mara Quill** - becomes an unwilling viewer after recovering a lantern

## Impact on Society

The archive turns private pain into political control. It also offers proof that official records cannot provide. Port Kestrel's central dilemma is not whether the archive lies. It is whether truth taken without consent can still be used to repair what the theft broke.
