# Port Kestrel

## Description

Port Kestrel is a raft-city of pontoons, causeways, algae tanks, and stacked housing modules moored above a drowned coastal town. The old church steeple still breaks the surface at low tide, green with weed and nesting gulls. Newer structures sit above it on steel legs and flotation drums, welded and rewelded until every rail carries three generations of repairs.

The harbor never stops moving. Walkways flex underfoot. Rainwater drums against plastic roofs. At night, the storm-grid lamps glow blue-white along the main piers and amber in the poorer docks where people repair the old bulbs instead of replacing them.

## History

After the second seawall failure, the remaining population converted the harbor into a floating settlement rather than abandon the shoals. The first years were improvised and brutal. Salvage crews stripped the drowned town for copper, medicine, batteries, and family keepsakes. The Harbor Council became the authority that rationed power, assigned mooring rights, and decided which ruins were safe to dive.

Three years ago, the Blackout Night killed twelve residents during a storm surge. The official report blamed Theo Quill for disabling the backup grid. The accusation never healed; it merely hardened into local fact.

## Culture & Customs

Residents measure status by usefulness. A person who can splice cable, patch hullskin, predict a pressure swing, or cook for a flooded dock will be forgiven almost anything. Funerals are held at the tide line, with names spoken once over water and once over the radio band reserved for emergencies.

People do not talk openly about memory therapy. Too many residents used it after storms, fires, and drownings. Too many came back from treatment lighter in ways their families could feel.

## Notable Features

- **Dock Six** - Mara's home dock, known for salvage crews and stubborn independence
- **The Storm Grid** - layered batteries, turbines, and breaker rafts that keep the city powered
- **Council Spire** - a dry, white administrative tower moored to the central pier
- **The Old Steeple** - the last visible piece of the drowned town beneath the harbor

## Current State

Port Kestrel is functioning but brittle. Storm seasons are worsening, salvage is thinning, and the Council's emergency powers have never fully ended. The town remembers the Blackout Night in public slogans and private silence.
