# Mara Quill

## Appearance

Compact, strong, and weather-marked from years working below the tide line. Her black hair is cropped short enough to fit cleanly under a dive hood. One ear carries a pressure scar from a bad ascent, and her hands are crosshatched with rope burns and coral cuts. She wears patched thermal layers, a yellow salvage harness, and Theo's old tide watch even though its casing is cracked.

## Personality & Traits

Mara is practical, suspicious, and difficult to impress. She trusts maintenance logs, tide tables, and the sound a winch makes when it is about to fail. She has little patience for public mourning, especially when the same town that brings casseroles still whispers Theo's name like a warning.

**Strengths:** Calm under pressure, excellent diver, mechanical intuition, hard to intimidate
**Flaws:** Withholds trust, treats grief as a task list, mistakes endurance for judgment

## Backstory

Mara and Theo grew up on Dock Six, raised by a mother who repaired storm shutters and taught both children how to read water. Mara became a salvage diver; Theo became a systems engineer for the Harbor Authority. On the Blackout Night, Port Kestrel lost power during a storm surge. Twelve people died, Theo among them. The Council blamed him for disabling the backup grid.

Mara survived because she was underwater, cutting loose a jammed intake gate. When she came up, the harbor was dark except for emergency flares and burning algae tanks. She has spent three years pretending the official report does not matter. It does.

## Motivations & Goals

**Primary:** Prove Theo did not sabotage Port Kestrel
**Secondary:** Protect Dock Six from Council retaliation
**Internal:** Admit that clearing Theo's name will not bring him back
**Fear:** That Theo was protecting something worse than his own reputation

## Voice & Speech Patterns

Plain and clipped when working. Uses practical comparisons and equipment language. Avoids sentimental phrasing, then says one painfully direct thing when her restraint breaks.

> "If the Council wanted the truth sealed, they should have used better bolts."

> "I am not digging him up. I am taking the knife out of his back."

## Character Arc

**Starting state:** Defines justice as clearing Theo's name at any cost
**Midpoint:** Learns the archive holds more than proof; it holds memories stolen from living residents
**End state:** Releases the truth with consent from the people most harmed, choosing repair over revenge
