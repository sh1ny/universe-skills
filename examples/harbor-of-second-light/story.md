# Harbor of Second Light

## Synopsis

After a storm exposes an illegal memory archive beneath a floating harbor, salvage diver Mara Quill finds a recording that proves her dead brother was not the saboteur everyone blames. To clear his name, she must decide whether to release the archive's stolen memories and wound the town again, or protect the lie that keeps Port Kestrel functioning.

## Tone & Style

Lean, salt-stung near-future mystery with a grounded coastal texture. The technology should feel repaired, reused, and embedded in daily work rather than sleek. The prose stays close to Mara's practical mind: precise about tools, tides, debt, and weather, less fluent around grief. Suspense comes from what the town has agreed not to remember.

## Setting

Port Kestrel is a floating harbor city moored above the ruins of an older drowned town. Its people survive on salvage, algae farms, storm forecasting, and tight mutual obligation. After the Blackout Night killed twelve residents and left Theo Quill blamed for sabotage, the Harbor Council rebuilt the official story around silence.

## Central Conflict

Mara needs the truth about Theo, but the only proof sits inside an archive built from stolen memories. Revealing it could clear him, expose Council crimes, and return private pain to people who paid to forget. Keeping it hidden would leave Theo buried under a lie.
